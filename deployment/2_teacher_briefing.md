## What it is

**WealthMind Africa** is a behavioural-economics platform I designed and built independently. Its core is a **2-minute, anonymous Financial Behaviour Assessment** that measures four things grounded in established economic theory:

| Measure | Economic basis |
|---|---|
| Financial Health Score | Composite index (the method used by the World Bank HDI / IMF soundness indicators) |
| Present Bias | Hyperbolic discounting — **Laibson (1997)** |
| Financial Resilience | Buffer-stock saving theory — **Deaton (1991)** |
| Savings Behaviour | Permanent Income Hypothesis — **Friedman (1957)** |

It is being used to run a small **research study**: *How does financial decision-making differ across age groups and life stages in Kenya?*

## What I actually did
- Built the full platform (assessment, scoring engine, anonymised analytics dashboard, public research report).
- Grounded every score in named economic models — one scoring engine, no invented metrics.
- Designed it **anonymous-by-default with explicit consent**; no names, emails, or identifying data are ever collected.
- Deployed it to family, my swim club, friends, and (with your help) the school, and analysed the results.

## How students participate (≈2 minutes)
1. Scan the QR / open the link.
2. Answer 8 questions about typical income, saving, spending timing, and resilience.
3. Consent screen — they choose whether their **anonymous** answers join the study.
4. They instantly see their own scores and a personalised economic insight.

No accounts, no homework, nothing graded. Entirely voluntary.

## Ethics (why this is safe to share with a class)
- Anonymous by default; **only consented responses are stored**; consent is one tap and reversible by simply declining.
- No identifying information is ever collected or shown.
- Only **aggregate** patterns are reported (groups of 8+), never an individual.
