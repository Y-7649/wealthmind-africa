# Economics Club Session — Slide Outline

**Format:** ~15 minutes + live participation. The session *is* a recruitment event: everyone takes the assessment in the middle, and you reveal the cohort's own data at the end. ~10 slides.

---

**Slide 1 — Title**
WealthMind Africa — *How do young Kenyans make money decisions?*
A behavioural-economics study. Your name. One line: "built it, deployed it, here's what the data says."

**Slide 2 — The question**
Economists predict specific patterns in financial behaviour. Do they actually show up in real people? Three concepts we'll test today:
- Present bias (Laibson) · Buffer-stock saving (Deaton) · Permanent income (Friedman).

**Slide 3 — Present bias, explained (30 sec)**
We value money *now* far more than money later — so spending spikes right after payday. "Have you ever spent big the day money arrived, then scraped by later?"

**Slide 4 — What I built**
A platform with a 2-minute, anonymous assessment that scores your financial behaviour on these exact models. One scoring engine; no invented metrics.

**Slide 5 — 🔴 TAKE IT NOW (the QR slide)**
Big QR code + link. "Scan it, 2 minutes, anonymous, you'll get your own scores. Choose to add your answer to our study at the end." *(Wait 3–4 minutes. This is the data-collection moment.)*

**Slide 6 — What your scores mean**
While they finish: walk through Financial Health, Present Bias, Resilience, Savings — what a high/low score implies.

**Slide 7 — 🟢 OUR live results**
Open the **Impact Report** on screen. "We just generated this dataset in 4 minutes." Show participant count + the present-bias %.

**Slide 8 — The interesting comparison**
Show the life-stage / age breakdown (students vs adults if available). "This is the actual research question — and here's what *our* data shows."

**Slide 9 — Why it matters**
Financial inclusion, M-Pesa, and why behaviour (not just access) determines outcomes — tie to Kenya. One real-world implication (commitment devices: M-Shwari, M-Akiba).

**Slide 10 — Take it home**
- Share the link with family + friends (QR again).
- "This is an ongoing study — the more participants, the stronger the finding."
- Contact + thanks.

---

**Speaker notes:**
- The energy peak is Slide 7 — generating real data live is the memorable moment. Rehearse the transition from "take it" → "here's us."
- Keep theory light and concrete; one relatable example per concept.
- End by asking 2–3 people to share to family that night (seeds the adult comparison group).
